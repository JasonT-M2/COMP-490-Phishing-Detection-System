Email Security Tips & Awareness

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing

1. Check the Sender's Email Address
Phishing emails often use addresses that look similar to legitimate companies. Always verify the domain name carefully. For example, attackers may replace letters with similar characters or add extra words to trick users.
2. Be Careful with Suspicious Links
Never click links from unknown senders. Hover over links before clicking to see the real destination. If the URL looks strange or does not match the company's official website, it may be a phishing attempt.
3. Watch for Urgent or Threatening Language
Phishing messages often create urgency such as “Your account will be suspended” or “Immediate action required.” These messages try to pressure users into making quick decisions without thinking.
4. Avoid Sharing Sensitive Information
Legitimate organizations will never ask for passwords, credit card numbers, or personal information through email. If you receive such requests, it is likely a phishing attempt.
5. Verify Attachments Before Opening
Attachments in phishing emails may contain malware or malicious scripts. Only open attachments from trusted senders and always scan files with antivirus software before opening them.
Security Reminder: When in doubt, do not click any links or download attachments. Report suspicious emails to your organization's security team or email provider.
